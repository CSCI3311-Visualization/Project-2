Topic 1: Tuition

How does the degree of urbanization affect tuition?

The degree of urbanization differs in every state but in general, colleges in the suburbs and in cities tend to be more expensive than colleges in rural places or towns.

What is the relationship between student’s financial aid and student loans?

The trend shows that there is a positive correlation between percent of students receiving financial aid and percent of students taking out loans. The more expensive colleges tend to offer less financial aid and have fewer students taking out loans.

How does the degree of urbanization play a role in students’ financial aid and loans?

Schools in the suburbs and cities offer less financial aid and loans than rural places and towns but also tend to have higher tuition prices.

Explanation

Tuition is a key factor in determining where a student ends up for college. We wanted to see how urbanization plays a role in tuition prices and how it differs in each state. The map allows us to compare the different types of college locations within each state since different states have a different range of urbanization colleges. The scatterplot shows the percent of students receiving financial aid and percent of students receiving loans. The darker dots in the scatterplot represent a higher tuition price. Putting these two ideas together, we get the last scatterplot which shows the relationship between percent of students who receive financial aid and percent of students with loans based on the college urbanization. The bigger size represents a higher tuition rate.
